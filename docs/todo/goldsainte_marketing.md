
# Goldsainte Marketing Notes + V2 Content Plan

Goal: improve traveller clarity + conversion.

**North Star:** A traveller browses **destinations + collections**, then **describes their ideal summer trip**, and Goldsainte matches them with the right **travel agent + creator** (or offers **off‚Äëthe‚Äëshelf packages/collections**) to get as close as possible to their described experience.

This document starts from the **current landing page** and proposes a **step-by-step evolution**: implement missing links first, then tighten value proposition and build education/conversion pages.

---

## 1) Quick Audit: current landing page (what a traveller understands)

### What‚Äôs working
- Premium/luxury tone is strong.
- ‚ÄúPost a dream trip‚Äù CTA is directionally aligned to ‚Äúdescribe ideal holiday‚Äù.
- The concept of a collaborative planning artifact (‚Äústoryboard‚Äù) is differentiated.

### What‚Äôs unclear (conversion friction)
- The main promise is implied, not stated:
	- *‚ÄúDescribe your ideal holiday ‚Üí we match you with an agent/creator OR show ready collections to book.‚Äù*
- ‚ÄúStoryboard‚Äù is a branded term; travellers may not instantly map it to ‚ÄúTrip brief / request‚Äù.
- The page highlights Creators/Agents/Brands heavily; travellers may wonder if the product is *for them* or for partners.
- ‚ÄúPackages‚Äù section reads like ‚ÄúStoryboards‚Äù (and currently shows placeholders). It does not communicate ‚Äúbrowse curated collections now‚Äù.
- Some CTAs/labels are confusing without explanation (example: ‚ÄúStart a storyboard with Madison‚Äù).

**Bottom line:** the page is visually compelling, but the traveller path is not yet spelled out in one clear sentence + 3 simple steps.

## 2) V2 Landing Page: Message Hierarchy (what we want a traveller to take away)

### Primary value proposition (plain language)
Use a simple, traveller-first sentence near the hero headline:

**Option A (direct):**
‚ÄúDescribe your ideal summer trip. We match you with a vetted travel agent + creator team ‚Äî or suggest curated collections you can book.‚Äù

**Option B (short):**
‚ÄúTell us what you want to feel. We‚Äôll build the trip.‚Äù

### Secondary value proposition (how it works)
Immediately follow with 3 steps:

1. **Browse** destinations + collections for inspiration
2. **Describe** your trip in a short brief (dates, vibe, budget, must‚Äëdos)
3. **Match + book** with the right agent + creator (or choose a ready collection)

### Proof / trust layer
Travellers need reassurance that this is real and safe:

- ‚ÄúVetted agents‚Äù (what that means)
- How payments work (escrow, refunds, etc. when applicable)
- Support availability
- Real examples (even 2‚Äì3 ‚Äúsample storyboards‚Äù)

### Terminology recommendation
Keep ‚ÄúStoryboard‚Äù as the brand word, but introduce it as:

**Storyboard = your trip brief (mood + must‚Äëdos + budget)**

This reduces cognitive load.
